## Executive Summary
This dashboard analyzes sales data from the Superstore dataset to provide insights into product performance, regional trends, and customer segmentation. It enables stakeholders to understand which areas drive the most sales, which products are in demand, and how performance varies over time.

## Key Findings

### 1. Sales Performance Over Time
- Total sales across 2017â€“2018 amounted to $392.88K.
- Highest monthly sales were recorded in April 2018; the lowest in December 2017.
- A clear upward trend is visible in Q2 of each year.

### 2. Regional Insights
- Coastal and Northeastern states (e.g., California, New York) are top contributors to sales.
- Midwest and Southern states show comparatively lower performance.

### 3. Customer Segmentation
- Corporate and Consumer segments contribute almost equally to total sales.
- Home Office segment represents a smaller share of the revenue.

### 4. Product Performance
- Technology category leads in overall sales, mainly driven by Phones and Machines.
- Furniture sales are dominated by Chairs and Tables.
- Office Supplies have a wide range of sub-categories but generate lower sales per item.

### 5. Top-Selling Products
- Phones, Chairs, and Machines are the top 3 best-performing products by sales value.
- Products in the Technology category have higher ticket sizes and more consistent demand.

## Strategic Recommendations

- **Focus marketing and inventory on top-performing states** to capitalize on existing demand.
- **Promote high-performing products** like Phones and Chairs in underperforming regions.
- **Investigate seasonal sales spikes** to optimize promotional calendars.
- **Consider bundling lower-selling Office Supplies** with high-selling items to boost their performance.
